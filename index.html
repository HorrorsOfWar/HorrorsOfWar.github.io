Overview

The Vietnam War, which began in 1954, was a long and costly conflict between the communist government of North Vietnam and South Vietnam (known as the Viet Cong) and its allies.

The war took over 3 million lives, which included more than 58,000 Americans, 1.1 million Viet Cong and North Vietnamese soldiers, approximately 521 Australians and more than half of the 3 million lives were Vietnamese civilians. U.S forces were forced to withdraw in 1973 due to the long and harsh battle. The war ended in 1975 with communist North Vietnam gaining control of South Vietnam and the country was unified as the Socialist Republic of Vietnam the following year.

Significant Dates of Vietnam

  • 1887 The French imposes a colonial system over Vietnam, calling it French Indochina.
  • 1923-1925 Ho Chi Minh, a Vietnamese nationalist is trained as a Communist International (Comitern) agent.
  • Feb 1930 The Indochinese Communist Party is founded by Ho Chi Minh.
  • Sep 1940 The Japanese invade French Indochina while there is little resistance from the French.
  • May 1941 Ho Chi Minh and communist colleagues aim to resist French and Japanese occupation of Vietnam.
  • Mar 1945 The Japanese declare Vietnam, Laos and Cambodia independent after carrying out a coup against French authorities.
  • Jul 1946 The Viet Minh begins a guerrilla war (an irregular war with small groups of soldiers) against the French after French proposal of granting Vietnam limited self-government.
  • Mar 1947 President Harry Truman declares that the United States is to assist any country threatened by communism, this foreign policy is known as the Truman Doctrine.
  • Jan 1950 The communist Democratic Republic of Vietnam is recognised by the Peopleâ€™s Republic of China and Soviet Union and economic and military aid was provided.
  • Feb 1950 The Viet Minh strengthens offensive against French outposts in Vietnam.
  • June 1950 The US identifies the Viet Minh as a communist threat and assists the French.
  • Mar-May 1954 French troops are defeated by Viet Minh forces at Dien Bien Phu.
  • July 1954 The Geneva Accords establish North and South Vietnam with the 17th parallel as the dividing line.
  • Sep 1960 Ho Chi Minh replaced by Le Duan as leader of North Vietnam's communist party due to Ho Chi Minh's health conditions.
  • Jan 1962 US aircraft started spraying Agent Orange and other herbicides over rural areas of South Vietnam to kill vegetation that provided cover and food for guerrilla forces.
  • Nov 1964 The Soviet Union increases military support to North Vietnam.
  • Jul 1965 President Johnson sends 50,000 more ground troops to be sent to Vietnam. Additionally increases draft to 35,000 each month.
  • 1966 US troop numbers exceed 400,000 in Vietnam.
  • Feb 1967 US aircraft bomb Haiphong Harbour and North Vietnamese Airfields.
  • Apr 1967 Vietnam War protests in Washington D.C., New York City and San Francisco.
  • Sep 1967 Nguyen Van Thieu wins the presidential election of South Vietnam.
  • Nov 1967 US suffer approximately 1,800 casualties in the Battle of Dak To.
  • Jan 1968 The Tet Offensive begins.
  • Mar 16 1968 US forces kill more than 500 civilians during search-and-destroy operations.
  • Nov 1968 Richard M. Nixon wins the US presidential elections promising law and order and to end the draft.
  • Sep 1969 Ho Chi Minh dies of a heart attack in Hanoi.
  • 1969-1972 Number of US forces in South Vietnam gradually reduced.
  • Mar 1969-May 1970 Secret bombings target suspected communist base camps.
  • Apr-Jun 1970 Communist bases across the Cambodian boarder attacked by US and South Vietnam forces.
  • Jan-Mar 1971 South Vietnam and US forces suffered heavy losses after forced to retreat in Operation Lam Son 1971.
  • Mar-Oct 1972 Large-scale, three-pronged Easter Offensive launched against South Vietnam and US forces, gaining control of more territory in South Vietnam.
  • Dec 1972 President Nixon orders roughly 20,000 tons of bombs over densely populated regions between Hanoi and Haiphong.
  • Jan 27 1973 The Selective Service announces the end to the draft.
  • Jan 27 1973 President Nixon signs the Paris Peace Accords, ending direct US involvement in the Vietnam War. While North Vietnam accepts cease fire, the North Vietnamese military officials continue plotting to overtake South Vietnam.
  • Feb-Apr 1973 North Vietnam releases 591 American prisoners of war; known as Operation Homecoming.
  • Aug 1974 President Nixon resigns after the Watergate Scandal is revealed. Gerald R. Ford becomes president.
  • Jan 1975 President Ford rules out any further US military involvement in Vietnam.
  • Apr 1975 During the Fall of Saigon, the capital of South Vietnam is seized by communist forces leading to the surrender of the government of South Vietnam.
  • Jul 1975 North and South Vietnam are officially unified as the communist Socialist Republic of Vietnam.
